Netizens: Students have no right to meddle in politics

By: Thea A. Racuya | 2019 May 12

With the upcoming Midterm Senatorial Elections just around the corner, social media netizens are unstoppable as soon as they start posting, sharing, and commenting on the hottest and latest issues in the political arena. For instance, several universities were roasted by the netizens for meddling in the political affairs of the country.

Retrieved from
https://web.facebook.com/OFFICIALSLUSSC

While almost all of the law schools or student governments of the different universities conducted mock senatorial elections, the University of the Philippines, Saint Louis University, and San Beda University specifically received the most backlash from the netizens. To sum up the results, majority of the population from all universities voted for the candidates of the Otso Diretso with Chel Diokno, Samira Gutoc, and Bam Aquino on the top (Lucas, 2019).
What made the polls more controversial were the comments of netizens saying 'wag kayong makisawsaw sa pulitika (don't meddle in politics). Aside from that statement, they also made fun of the students and claimed that they were still too young (masyado pa kayong mga bata) . Indeed, social media is a platform to freely express our feelings and opinions but that freedom is inseparable with responsibility and consequence.

Retrieved from
https://web.facebook.com/TheBarristerSBCLaw

Personally, as a member of the youth, being told to stay out of politics basically means dropping my right to be informed of the issues of our home country. Politics is more than just governmental dynamics, it is how the elements of the state work together to ponder on societal, political, and economic issues. Mind you that two of the elements are the government and the people. It's true that the people of my generation still have a long way to go in order to catch up with the millennials' and generation x's experience and practical wisdom, but it doesn't mean that we cannot keenly observe and comprehend what's happening around us.
Not to generalize, but the people who tell children to go to school and use what they've learned to raise awareness don't even listen to them after they've become educated. What's the purpose of exerting effort in learning contemporary issues when it's just going to be left in school after graduation?
In the 2016 elections, 45.49% of registered voters belong to the youth age group (Lucas, 2019). Recent statistics from the mock elections essentially shows the trends and characteristics of the youth. They do vote differently from the rest of the population#rejecting the status quo. Maybe that's the reason why they were bashed by fanatics. There is no doubt that the votes from the youth is also important in determining the next set of leaders.

So, what do I say? What do we say? NO. We will not stop meddling in politics. It is duly our right to be informed of progresses and events that will either lead to the downfall or success of our country. Exercising the salient features of democratic relations between us fellow citizens serves as the backbone of a possible change we needed since the invasion of foreign colonizers. Ignorance is not an excuse when we can choose 'not' to stay ignorant.
